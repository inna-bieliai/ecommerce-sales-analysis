# 📋 Business Insights — E-commerce Sales Analysis

## Dataset Overview
- **Period:** January 2014 — December 2017
- **Total Sales:** $2,297,220
- **Total Profit:** $286,396
- **Overall Profit Margin:** 12.5%
- **Total Orders:** 5,009
- **Total Customers:** 793

---

## 1. Category & Product Performance

### Findings
- **Technology** is the most profitable category — 17.4% margin ($145,455 profit)
- **Office Supplies** follows closely — 17.0% margin ($122,491 profit)
- **Furniture** is critically underperforming — only 2.5% margin ($18,451 profit)

### Sub-category highlights
| Sub-Category | Total Sales | Profit Margin |
|---|---|---|
| Labels | $12,486 | 44.4% ✅ |
| Copiers | $149,528 | 37.2% ✅ |
| Tables | $206,966 | -8.6% 🔴 |
| Bookcases | $114,880 | -3.0% 🔴 |
| Supplies | $46,674 | -2.6% 🔴 |

### Recommendation
Discontinue or reprice Tables and Bookcases. Despite high sales volume, both sub-categories consistently generate losses.

---

## 2. Sales Trends & Seasonality

### Findings
- Sales grew from $484K (2014) to $733K (2017) — strong upward trend
- **2015 anomaly:** Sales dipped -2.83% YoY, but profit actually increased — suggesting the company sold fewer but more profitable products or reduced discounting
- **2016 recovery:** +29.47% YoY growth — strongest year
- **2017:** +20.36% YoY growth — sustained momentum

### Seasonality pattern
- **Peak months:** September, November, December (Q4 budget spending)
- **Weakest month:** February — consistently lowest every year
- **Negative profit months:** July 2014 (-$841), January 2015 (-$3,281)

### Recommendation
Plan inventory and promotions around Q4 peak. Avoid aggressive discounting in already weak months (February, January) as it compounds losses.

### Findings

| East | $678,781 | 13.5% ✅ |
| Central | $501,240 | 7.9% ⚠️ |

### State-level insights
- **Top profitable states:** California ($76,381), New York ($74,038), Washington ($33,402)
- **Loss-making states:** Texas (-$25,729), Ohio (-$16,971), Pennsylvania (-$15,560)
- **Texas paradox:** $170K in sales but -15.1% margin — heavy discounting is the likely cause

### Recommendation
Investigate discount practices in Texas, Ohio, and Illinois. These states have strong sales volumes but are destroying profitability.

---

## 4. Customer Segments

### Findings
| Segment | Total Sales | Profit Margin | Avg Order Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer | $1,161,400 | 11.6% | $449 |
| Corporate | $706,147 | 13.0% | $466 |
| Home Office | $429,653 | 14.0% | $473 |

- **Home Office** is the most efficient segment — smallest volume but highest margin and highest average order value
- **Consumer** drives the most revenue but at the lowest margin

### Customer highlight — Sean Miller
- #1 customer by sales ($25,043) but **unprofitable** (-$1,980 loss)
- Root cause: 50% discount on a single Cisco TelePresence unit ($22,638) wiped out all profit from his 15 purchases
- This illustrates how a single large discount can negate an entire customer relationship's value

### Recommendation
Shift focus toward Corporate and Home Office segments. Implement per-customer discount caps to prevent high-value orders from becoming loss-makers.

---

## 5. Discount Impact Analysis

### Key finding
Discounts above **20% consistently generate losses** across all categories.

| Discount Level | Total Profit |
|---|---|
| 0% | +$320,987 ✅ |
| 20% | +$90,337 ✅ |
| 30% | -$10,369 🔴 |
| 50% | -$20,506 🔴 |
| 70% | -$40,075 🔴 |
| 80% | -$30,539 🔴 |

### Top loss-making products (all heavily discounted)
1. Cubify CubeX 3D Printer — 53% avg discount → -$8,880 loss
2. Lexmark Laser Printer — 40% avg discount → -$4,590 loss
3. Cubify 3D Printer Triple Head — 50% avg discount → -$3,840 loss

### Recommendation
**Implement a strict 20% discount cap.** Any discount above this threshold requires manager approval. The data clearly shows that discounts above 20% destroy profitability regardless of sales volume.
